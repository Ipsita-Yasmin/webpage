Graduate Studies - Master of Data Science

Master's Program for Experts

In this expert master's program, you'll acquire the expertise to predict the future, shape strategies, and guide decision-making. By mastering data management, analysis, and processing, you'll tackle global challenges and improve lives. Collaborating with top IT professionals at University of Regina, you'll gain cutting-edge knowledge and develop a mindset for positive impact.

What do we offer ?

  • Gain expertise in evaluating the complete data lifecycle within organizations.
  • Apply foundational data analysis and exploration theories to address typical challenges.
  • Excel in formulating data science projects for innovative application domains.
  • Proficiently investigate, document, and communicate prerequisites for global data analysis enhancement.
  • Demonstrate a deep understanding of data science at senior professional standards.
  • Critically assess, evaluate, and apply contemporary data science theories.
  • Develop skills for both professional and academic contexts.
  • Effectively document and communicate ethical and legal considerations in data science practice, especially regarding privacy and security.

Key Highlights

Overview

Admissions: Fall semester intake only (annual cohort).


Application Deadline: February 15. Missed deadline? Join the next year's cohort.


Curriculum: Five-semester program covering data science topics.


Key Courses: Software development, Python and data fundamentals, data science foundations, statistics, machine learning, applied machine learning, big data analytics, and communication skills.


Co-op Option: Available after specific course completion with a high average grade.


Work Terms: Fifth and sixth semesters, potential to work with different employers.

Work Term Credits: Additional to program credit requirements.

Academic Performance: Minimum GPA requirement, specific to the program.

Prerequisite Courses: Some programs may require specific prerequisites.

Recommendation Letters: Letters of recommendation from professors or employers may be needed.

Statement of Purpose: A statement outlining academic and career goals is often required.

English Language Proficiency: Proficiency tests like TOEFL or IELTS for non-native English speakers.

Standardized Tests: Check if GRE scores are required.

Resume/CV: A detailed resume or CV may be requested.

These criteria can vary by program, so it's essential to verify the specific requirements for the Master's in Data Science program at the University of Regina through their official website or admissions department.

Course Structure

To earn a Master's in Data Science with a Co-op Designation, students must complete 30 credits of coursework. However, before they can start their co-op work terms, they must finish the following courses: CS 700, 710, 711, 712, 713, 714, 715, and 716.

Scholarship

NSERC offers scholarships to exceptional Canadian citizens and Permanent Residents in natural sciences and engineering. Master's students receive up to $17,500 for one year, and doctoral students get up to $35,000 per year for three years through an online application process. CGS goes to the top-ranked applicants, and PGS supports the next tier. This funding allows scholars to focus on their studies and find research mentors. Co-op Facts & Perks

A co-op designation may be added to this graduate program, under the following conditions:

  • An application to the co-op designation may be made at the end of the third semester (after the completion of CS 700, 710, 711, 712, 713, and 714).
  • A minimum average grade of 80% or higher must have been achieved on the courses taken in the first three semesters of the program.
  • The arrangement of a co-op placement will be done in collaboration with the Career Centre during the fourth semester.
  • A minimum average grade of 80% or higher must be maintained through the fourth semester of the program (CS 715, 716).
  • The co-op placements will occur during the fifth and sixth semesters; these can be with the same employer or different ones.
  • If two semesters of work terms are completed, a co-op designation will be added to your academic record.
  • The normal coursework of the fifth semester (CS 719) will take place after the end of the co-op placements (in the sixth or seventh semester, depending on whether one or two co-op terms are taken).

NOTE: Approval from the Co-op Grad Coordinator is necessary for Co-op work term acceptance, which is a competitive process through the Career Centre, and the credit hours earned are extra for this program.
